Beastmen cities receive 1/2 power/population and their military units have 1 more health and attack. Beastmen can build most buildings.

Dark elf grow slowly, have high resistance, generate 1 power per population, and their military units all have ranged magical attacks. Dark elves can build everything except Maritime Guilds.

Draconian cities receive 1/2 power/population, and their military units can fly. They also have a good resistance, and can breath fire.

=== DWARF TOWNSFOLK ===
Dwarves double mineral bonuses and pay 50% more taxes. Their units have high resistance, and 1 additional health. Dwarves can't build Stables, Colosseum, Magic Markets, Sage's Guilds, Amplifying Towers, Merchant's Guilds, Banks, and their population grows very slowly.

=== GNOLL TOWNSFOLK ===
Gnolls are physically stronger, having +2 attack per unit, +1 movement and +1 resistance. They cannot build advanced buildings, and Magic Markets.

=== HALFLING TOWNSFOLK ===
Halflings can build all non-military buildings but their military is limited. Halflings are open-minded and curious, producing 1 research per population. Halfling units, albeit weak, are lucky, which makes them harder to kill.

=== HIGH ELF TOWNSFOLK ===
High elf cities grow very slowly, but generate 0.5 power/population. They have forester, +1 to hit, and can build everything except Maritime Guilds and Colosseums. High Elf units also have 1 higher resistance than usual.

High men are a versatile race good at everything. They are masters of architecture, paying 15% less for producing buildings, but their units have very low magic resistance.

Klackons grow the fastest, and their military units have an extra 2 defense. Klackons cannot build Mechanicians Guilds, Shrines, War Colleges, Fantastic Stables, Maritime Guilds, Banks and Merchant's Guilds.

Lizardmen units can move in water, and have +1 health but -1 Resistance. Lizardmen cannot build advanced buildings, War Colleges, and Miner's Guilds. Lizardmen settlers move faster.

Nomad cities receive a +50% trade bonus that is treated as a road gold bonus, and cannot build Granaries. Nomad units have +3 resistance. Basic nomad units are equipped with a bow.

=== ORC TOWNSFOLK ===
Orcs can build every building, and have a high population growth. They are average at everything, with no specific advantages or disadvantages worth mentioning, but their units are cheaper to maintain than usual.

=== TROLL TOWNSFOLK ===
Troll units can regenerate, have higher attack strength and health, but fewer figures and are more expensive. Trolls can build everything except advanced buildings and Sage's Guilds.

Dwarves generate twice the revenue of other races and pay double the specified tax rate without an increase in the unrest. Each city's gold income is also affected by the presence of shores, rivers, and a marketplace, bank, or merchant's guild.

=== ADVISORS ===
This list allows you to select a specialist to give you information about your progress and environment.

=== FAME ===
Fame decreases the total amount a wizard pays for troop maintenance by one gold for each fame point. In addition, more powerful heroes will only approach wizards with higher fame.
 A wizard may obtain fame from being famous, hiring legendary heroes, winning a major battle, building colosseums, or by capturing a village, city or capitol. Likewise, any wizard losing a major battle will lose fame.

=== NIGHTSHADE ===
Nightshade is a magical plant that protects the city from spells. Each plant adds +100 dispel strength against any bad city enchantment. Furthermore, Cathedrals produce 3 additional power for each Nightshade, and reduce 1 additional unrest.

=== MANA LEAK ===
All enemy spell casters are drained 5 mana each combat turn. This includes the opponent wizard you are currently battling. Additionally, all units with magical ranged attacks lose one spell shot each turn.

=== PLAGUE ===
The virulent plague kills one population each turn if the current population exceeds a 1-10 roll. There is a 5% chance each turn that the plague will cease.

=== POPULATION BOOM ===
The population boom doubles the population growth rate of a city. The boom will continue until a 5% chance roll cancels the event.

=== BLUE CONJUNCTION ===
The rising triad of blue stars come together, doubling all power gained from sorcery nodes and halving all others. There is a 5% chance each turn that the conjunction will end. All power spent towards increasing casting skill has 3 times the normal effect. AEther Binding is 3 times more powerful as well.

=== RED CONJUNCTION ===
The rising triad of red stars come together, doubling all power gained from chaos nodes and halving all others. There is a 5% chance each turn that the conjunction will end. All single target direct damage combat spells are 33% more powerful. Note that life draining is not a direct damage effect, but a curse.

=== GREEN CONJUNCTION ===
The rising triad of green stars come together, doubling all power gained from nature nodes and halving all others. There is a 5% chance each turn that the conjunction will end. All non-Death fantastic creatures have +2 defense and resistance in battle.

=== BAD MOON ===
The moon controlling the powers over evil waxes, increasing the power from bad temples by 100% while halving the power obtained from good temples. There is a 5% chance each turn that the event will cease, returning power levels to normal. All units lose 2 resistance during combat.

=== GOOD MOON ===
The moon controlling the powers over good waxes, increasing the power from good temples by 100% while halving the power obtained from bad temples. There is a 5% chance each turn that the event will cease, returning power levels to normal. During the event, unit enchantments cost 50% less to cast.

=== AEther Flux ===
The fabric of magic is overflowing with power. All spells cost 50% less to cast.

=== POPULATION ===
This is the actual population of the city and the population growth rate shown in parenthesis. Each townsfolk figure represents 1000 townsfolk. The growth rate is how many people the city grows each turn.

=== DEATH SPELLS ===
Death spells provide the summoning and controlling of the dead and the forces of darkness. Death magic has powerful enchantments that strike disease and misfortune upon their enemies. Death books shift the wizard's alignment towards evil.

=== CHAOS SPELLS ===
Chaos spells deal primarily with corruption and destruction, calling upon the elemental forces of change. Chaos books shift the wizard's alignment towards evil.

=== LIFE SPELLS ===
Life spells focus on healing, protections and inspirational enchantments. Life books shift the wizard's alignment towards Good.

=== NATURE SPELLS ===
Nature spells control the raw forces of earth and water, as well as fertility and growth. Nature books shift the wizard's alignment towards good.

=== SORCERY SPELLS ===
Sorcery spells focus on elemental forces of air and illusion. Sorcery also has a number of meta-magic spells that resist and control other wizard's spells. Sorcery books have no effect on a wizard's alignment.

=== MANIACAL ===
Maniacal wizards hate everyone, reducing positive diplomatic effects by 25% and increasing negative ones by 50%. There is absolutely no positive effect from this personality on diplomacy, and they are the most fond of casting overland spells that bring harm to others.

=== RUTHLESS ===
Ruthless wizards like casting spells that damage the resources of other wizards. They are hard to deal with, but not entirely impossible, adding only +10 points to each diplomatic decision. These wizards don't care much about dispelling city curses.

=== AGGRESSIVE ===
Aggressive wizards are more likely to go to war than peaceful or lawful wizards, but less likely than chaotic, maniacal or ruthless ones. You can still reason with them through diplomacy and treaties, adding a medicore +20 points to their diplomatic decisions.

=== CHAOTIC ===
Chaotic wizards are totally unpredictable. One turn the chaotic may be peaceful and non-violent, while the next the wizard will go to war over any little excuse. Unlike other personalities, Chaotic wizards have a random diplomacy modifier that is re-rolled every turn in the range of +1 to +50.

=== LAWFUL ===
Lawful wizards will never attack anyone's cities that they are on good terms with. Negative diplomatic actions, however, have double effectiveness with them. Any diplomatic decision they make is affected by a +40 point modifier, making it much more likely for the wizard to be understanding of the good intentions of others. These wizards don't tolerate city curses and will dispel them as soon as possible.

=== PEACEFUL ===
Peaceful wizards are eager to maintain peaceful relations even after being attacked. They will not attack your towns unless provoked, even if there is no treaty preventing it. Diplomatic decisions made by this wizard suffer from a +65 positive modifier, making it quite easy to manipulate such wizards in your favor, although this modifier has a reversed effect if a negative action is requested from them, like breaking treaties.

=== PRAGMATIST ===
The pragmatist seeks a balance between military buildup, expansion, and spell research.

=== MILITARIST ===
The militarist seeks to build great and powerful armies. The wizard will build and maintain large and powerful armies, and is likely to declare war on wizards weaker than themselves without provocation. Their preferred types of spells are summoning and unit enchantments.

=== THEURGIST ===
The theurgist will focus resources on the development of their magic powers and spell research. They prefer casting global and city enchantments more than others.

=== PERFECTIONIST ===
The perfectionist wishes to develop and maintain huge prosperous cities. The wizard will concentrate on creating new buildings and city improvements. The perfectionist prefers casting city and unit enchantments most, and doesn't tolerate curses affecting their cities.

=== EXPANSIONIST ===
The expansionist is primarily interested in expanding controlled territory. The wizard is more interested in building naval buldings, and is likely to declare war on weaker wizards without a provocation. Their preferred types of spells are summoning, and spells that hurt enemy units, cities or wizards directly, softening them before conquest. They avoid wasting resouces on dispelling city curses and prefer to conquer more cities instead.

=== WIZARD PACT ===
A Wizard's Pact is an agreement of non-agression. You agree not to move within 2 squares of any of this wizard's city. In exchange, neither side can attack any units owned by the other unless the treaty is broken. A wizard's pact improves relations over time.

=== ALLIANCE ===
An alliance is a symbol of mutual trust and shared interests. You are allowed to move through their territories, even with military units. An alliance grants a higher chance of your requests being accepted, and improves relations over time twice faster than a Wizard's Pact. Additionally, if you defeat units owned by your ally's enemy, they will reward you with gold, provided they can afford to do so. This gold will immediately transfer to your treasury, but you'll only be notified about the transfer during your ally's turn.

=== NO REPORT ===
You are unable to determine what units this city has in its garrison because the city is beyond your detection range.

=== MOVEMENT ANIMATIONS ===
Controls whether or not troops in combat will animate while moving from square to square.

=== BLUR ===
Blurs the outline all friendly units making them more difficult to hit. There is a 20% chance that each point of damage done will miss.

You receive 100 points for every wizard that you banished.

The MP gained this way cannot exceed the maximum MP capacity of the hero. Super Battlemage (*) increases this effect by 50%.

=== SUPPLY COMMANDER ===
