**Animate Dead** is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant") of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). It may only be cast in battle. It has a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50**,
and requires there to be at least one unit that has perished during
this specific engagement and can be affected by the spell. **Animate Dead** returns the targeted unit as an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") creature with full [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** and [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.

Although the unit is supposed to carry back to the overland map if it survives the battle, this does not actually happen for **Animated** enemy units in the latest official version of the game. This spell also suffers from other [major bugs](#Known_Bugs), and can completely destabilize or even crash the game.

## Effects

**Animate Dead** fills the
corpses of a dead unit with dark energy. This allows the caster to
manipulate these corpses, forcing them to move and fight under their
command. The result is an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")
unit with similar capabilities to the original (previously dead) one,
which may last until the end of the battle or even beyond.

### Valid Targets

In order to cast **Animate Dead**, at least one *valid*
target must have been completely destroyed previously in the same
battle. As long as all units are still alive on the battlefield, the
spell may not be cast. While most units can be **Animated** regardless of who their original owner was, there are a few exceptions - [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures, [combat summons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell#Combat_Summoning_Spells "Summoning Spell"), and units slain mostly by [Irreversible Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points#Irreversible_Damage_Points "Damage Points") may not be targeted with this spell. Units with [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") can be brought back if they originally belonged to the caster, but not if they were enemy units.

### Restoring the Dead

When **Animate Dead** is
successfully cast, the game will present a list of the available targets
to the player in a pop-up window. Once a unit is selected, it is
returned to the battlefield with a short animation identical to the one
seen when casting [Summoning Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell "Summoning Spell")
during combat. However, the tile on which the unit appears can not be
chosen by the caster, and is instead picked randomly by the computer
from a 3 × 3 area near the middle of the map, that corresponds to the
side that cast the spell.

The unit is immediately restored to its maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** and [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count, along with a full [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance")
for the turn, and may instantly move, attack, or use spells and
abilities if it has any. It retains all of the attributes and properties
it had before being slain, although if it had any [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), those are lost upon death, as are any mutations gained through [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** or [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Doom Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Mastery "Doom Mastery")**. In addition, the unit is now [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), gaining both the positive and negative perks associated with this attribute.

### Undead Units

:   *Main article: [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")*

Being [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")
is a property that is neither an ability nor an enchantment. It could
best be described as a curse, but it's not exactly that either - it can
not be removed by any means, short of the complete destruction of the
unit. Undead require no sustenance, and are unquestioningly loyal.
Normal Units that become Undead no longer have an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") in either [Food](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Food "Food")**Food** or [Gold](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gold "Gold") **Gold**. However, in the case of [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"),
the magic that binds them to the material world actually becomes harder
to maintain. Undead versions of such units therefore have an *increased* Upkeep Cost that is **150%**
of their original. Granted, these changes are only relevant if the unit
survives the battle and can be carried back to the overland map.

Undead units retain all of the abilities and numeric attributes
they had in life - including, in the case of Normal Units, any benefits
they have already gained from [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience**. However, they do stop accumulating any more [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience Points**, and can thus gain no more [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Levels**. Undead are always considered to be Fantastic Units associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), and effects that apply to- or against [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures will all now treat them as such, including the fact that if they are destroyed again, they can not be **Animated** any more. Certain magics of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** Realm are also extra effective against Undead units.

Undead gain some of the signature immunities of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** units, although it should be noted that this feature is bugged in the official game, and most of these will actually *not*
be applied to the unit despite the game documentation and the in-game
help claiming otherwise. The only one that does get added is [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity") [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity"), which renders these units impervious to many spells and [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** Realm. Being Undead also has one more significant drawback. It disables [natural healing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points#Natural_Healing "Damage Points"), and nullifies the effect of most healing spells as well. Undead can only restore lost [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** in one of two ways: by dealing [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage"), or by possessing the [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") ability.

### At the End of Combat

| This article/section is missing crucial information and may require additional research to answer the following questions:   *** How exactly does the official game decide whether an **Animated** unit is carried back to the overland map?*** **How does the [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") ownership fix affect this circumstance?** |

According to the game's documentation, **Animate Dead**
is supposed to bring its target back in a persistent fashion. That is,
if the unit survives the battle, it should carry back to the overland
map under the command of the caster in all cases. Unfortunately, this
does not quite work as intended in the latest official version of the
game. First and foremost, the ownership of the unit is only set for the
duration of the battle. That is, if it was an enemy unit, and it does
make it back to the overland map, it will still belong to the enemy!

Whether the unit is carried over to the campaign map is a bit of a
mystery. Current theory suggests that this has something to do with the
ownership of the unit. If the player' own unit is animated, and they
win the battle, it will typically remain in play if it survived.
However, if it was an enemy unit before, it will generally disappear if
the caster was controlling the defending force, but not if they were the
attacker which, considering that the unit does not switch sides
overland, can mean that there will be units of two different players on
the same map tile.

In any case, since the unit is now [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), it will have a different [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") than before. This is important in the case of Undead [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), as they cost 50% more [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
to maintain, and are often actually not worth keeping because of that,
since the benefits don't typically make up for this. On the other hand, [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") will have no Upkeep Cost whatsoever when transformed into Undead.

## Usage

 [The target-selection menu for Animate Dead shows all valid targets on the battlefield.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/4/45/Screenshot_AnimateDead.png/revision/latest?cb=20120316152030)

The target-selection menu for **Animate Dead** shows all valid targets on the battlefield.



**Animate Dead** may only be cast during combat, for a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50**.
Upon its successful casting, the game will display a list of all valid
targets (see above for exceptions) to select from, listed in the order
in which they died - the first unit that died is listed at the top. If
no units have yet fallen during the current battle, or none of the ones
that did are valid targets, the game will not allow **Animate Dead** to be selected from the spellbook.

When the spell is cast, a blue pentagram will appear on the
battlefield in a tile randomly selected out of a 3 × 3 area
corresponding to the caster's side of the battlefield, just up and
towards the right from the middle. The **Animated** unit rises from this, much like a summoned unit, and is ready for action as soon as the animation has finished.

## Acquisition

As a [Very Rare Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), **Animate Dead** may become available to any [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who acquires at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**3** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
With any less, the spell can normally not be learned during the
campaign. This is the minimum requirement for it to be traded for,
appear in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be found in the spoils of victory when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival Wizard who already knows it.

The base chance for **Animate Dead** to be [researchable](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research "Spell Research") (at some point) in the campaign is roughly 10% (with [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**3** books), which gradually increases with the amount of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** Spellbooks possessed or found during gameplay. With [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**10** or more, the spell is certain to show up sooner or later, unless acquired from another source. It has a [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research#Research_Cost "Spell Research") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **3,000**, although its research is quicker for Wizards possessing the [Sage Master](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sage_Master "Sage Master") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), or a bookshelf containing [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**8** or more.

**Animate Dead** may not be acquired at the start of the game, regardless of the amount of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**
books selected from the starting picks. It must always be either
researched or found. However, a bug exists in the official game, that
may occasionally allow **Animate Dead** to be researched with only [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**2** books despite its [Rarity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Rarity "Spell Rarity"). This can happen if, and only if, the second [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**
Spellbook comes from Treasure, as it then allows a random Very Rare
Spell to be researched, which has a flat 10% chance of being this spell.
This oversight is corrected in the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50").

In addition, **Animate Dead** is available as a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") spell. It is known by [Ravashack the Necromancer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ravashack_the_Necromancer "Ravashack the Necromancer"), although it's worth noting that this feature is bugged in the latest official version of the game, and **Animate Dead**
is one of the few affected spells. This means that Ravashack will
likely not be able to actually access this spell unless the player
installs one of the unofficial patches. Even then, he can't cast it at
his initial level either. He needs to become at least a [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Commander** (level 4), become a [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Captain** (level 3) if *both* of his [Random Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Random_Abilities "Random Abilities") grant extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**, or otherwise equip [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") to gain enough [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** to use **Animate Dead**.

## Strategy

**Animate Dead** can be a
good way to either try to "save" a valuable unit from death, or to
ensure that an army can continue fighting despite its losses. It can be
considered as an impromptu version of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels")**, which transforms a unit into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") one on the overland map, but has both additional benefits and disadvantages compared to that spell. For one, **Animate Dead** can be cast on a dead [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") to turn it into an Undead unit, which is something that cannot be accomplished with [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels")**.

However, considering the increased [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of such a unit, and the fact that [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures may not be **Animated**,
this is more of a situational advantage. On the other hand, being able
to bring back one of the opponent's units can dramatically change the
outcome of a battle, as it may allow for strategies that would otherwise
be unavailable to the caster. Naturally though, the unit must still be
destroyed first before **Animate Dead** can be cast on it.

## Known Bugs

**Animate Dead** is
unfortunately one of the most bug-stricken spells in the latest official
version of the game. Selecting it for casting when there are more than 6
available targets will cause an immediate crash. This is made worse by
the fact that the spell also treats "uninvolved" units as possible
targets - for example, ground units being transported by a ship during
an [Ocean](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ocean "Ocean") battle, where they could normally not participate since they would be unable to move on the map.

Casting **Animate Dead**
may also cause the graphics to break, and units to get displayed at
their past location(s) in addition to where they currently are. Although
the documentation claims that **Animated** units that survive the
battle can be kept by the caster, this does not always happen either. In
the case of enemy units, even if they do make it back to the overland
map, the program fails to set their new owner, meaning that they will
still belong to the opponent.

AI [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard")
are unable to use this spell at all, so there is no need to worry about
them trying to cast it and thereby breaking the game. However, this
does strip them of a potentially useful [Very Rare Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell"). Although the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50")
corrects all of the bugs that result in crashes or graphical glithces,
it also completely removes the intended effect of being able to keep the
**Animated** unit in the process, regardless of who its original owner was.
